// What is the SIL

A knowledge base
for every sector.

The Systemic Intelligence Library (SIL) is the foundational knowledge layer of the c-ECO Framework. Each SIL document is a structured, research-verified dossier for one of the 17 global sectors monitored by c-ECO — providing the empirical substance that calibrates TFP instruments.

Function 01
TFP Calibration Substrate

SIL indicators directly populate the P (Position), ΔV (Velocity), σ (Uncertainty), and Lr (Reversibility) variables used to compute the TDR Systemic Proximity Score for each sector.

Function 02
Sector Risk Intelligence

Each SIL maps 30 primary systemic risks, secondary amplification mechanisms, and a cascade taxonomy — the cross-sector failure pathways that conventional sector analysis ignores.

Function 03
Legal & Governance Architecture

Applicable international treaties, national frameworks, liability cascade design, and data sovereignty conflicts — the institutional landscape that defines where TFP instruments can and cannot operate.

Function 04
Stress Test & Recovery

Five stress test scenarios per sector with Monte Carlo simulation parameters, Black Start recovery timelines, spare parts inventories, and mutual aid agreement architectures.

// Document Structure

Nine parts.
Every sector.

Each SIL document follows a standardised nine-part architecture, ensuring consistent calibration inputs across all sectors and enabling cross-sector cascade analysis.

Part I
Domain Intelligence Profile

Sector definition and scope, historical evolution timeline for ΔV calibration, global institutional landscape.

Part II
Systemic Risk Intelligence

30 primary systemic risks with TFP variable linkage, secondary amplification mechanisms, emerging risk horizon scan, and cascade taxonomy across all 17 sectors.

Part III
Systemic Response Intelligence

Pre-positioned response protocols by SPS Level, Calibration Council activation matrix, communication blackout contingencies.

Part IV
Governance & Legal Architecture

Applicable international treaties, national regulatory frameworks (10 jurisdictions), liability cascade architecture, data sovereignty and extraterritoriality conflicts.

Part V
TFP Calibration Intelligence

30 Position (P) indicators with SPS L3 thresholds and data sources; Velocity (ΔV) rate-of-change indicators; Uncertainty (σ) confidence intervals; Reversibility Liquidity (Lr) capacity scores.

Part VI
Scenario & Simulation Intelligence

Five stress test scenarios with full TFP variable values, Monte Carlo simulation parameters (10,000 iterations), Black Start recovery timeline.

Part VII
Restoration & Recovery Architecture

Restoration provider certification criteria, recovery sequencing with RTO targets, spare parts inventory requirements, mutual aid agreement architecture.

Part VIII
Authoritative Source Library

Source classification (Class A — institutional primary, Class B — industry standard, Class C — academic/NGO), cross-referenced by TFP variable.

Part IX
Source Matrices & Historical Intelligence

Source-to-variable matrix, source-to-risk matrix, monitoring gap analysis, dependency matrix, and 60-event historical intelligence library for ΔV calibration.
