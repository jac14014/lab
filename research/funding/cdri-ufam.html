Main Working Brief
Main Briefing

Manaus Urban Infrastructure Resilience

Working concept for joint development
Executive Summary

This meeting aims to assess the technical, institutional, and operational feasibility of jointly developing a proposal for the CDRI Urban Infrastructure Resilience Programme — Brazil 2026.

Manaus is presented as the initial priority territory because of its population, economic and logistical importance, exposure to hydrological and climate risks, and ongoing institutional planning processes that may be strengthened.

The initial hypothesis is to develop an urban infrastructure decision-support protocol that integrates existing hydrological, climate, operational, exposure, and service data and converts them into predefined procedures for planning, preparedness, and continuity of critical services.

Potential Scope
  • Urban drainage
  • Water supply and sanitation
  • Mobility and logistics
  • Continuity of essential services
  • Social infrastructure
  • Risk communication
  • Protection of vulnerable populations
  • Institutional decision-making
Working Principle
Hazard Threshold + Exposure + Vulnerability + Service Consequence + Predefined Institutional Action
Priority Territory

Manaus is the proposed principal city. Manacapuru, Tabatinga, or Tefé may be considered only as limited validation or replication areas, subject to local partners, urban justification, logistics, and endorsements.

Problem Formulation

Weak formulation: Manaus needs a protocol to monitor river levels.

Stronger formulation: The agencies responsible for urban planning, drainage, mobility, water supply, sanitation, and continuity of essential services in Manaus lack a common institutional process that converts hydrological forecasts and risk data into predefined infrastructure decisions.

Discussion Draft — Non-Binding
Draft Memorandum of Understanding

UFAM–c-ECO Cooperation Framework

Discussion draft only. Subject to UFAM’s internal review, legal analysis, competent-authority approval, and adaptation to the University’s official cooperation template.

Purpose

To establish a preliminary framework for scientific, technical, educational, and institutional cooperation in infrastructure resilience, climate adaptation, disaster risk reduction, environmental governance, decision-support systems, artificial intelligence, and public-sector capacity building.

Initial CDRI Cooperation

The parties may jointly assess and develop a proposal under the CDRI Urban Infrastructure Resilience Programme — Brazil 2026, including concept development, methodology, Theory of Change, work planning, stakeholder engagement, budgeting, governance, sustainability, and proposal documentation.

Role of the CCA

Following referral by the UFAM Rector’s Office, the Center for Environmental Sciences may serve as the academic focal point for initial technical discussions, subject to internal approvals and formal designation.

Financial Understanding

This Memorandum does not provide for transfer of funds, procurement, employment, or binding financial commitments. Any funded activity must be governed by a separate agreement and approved work plan.

Intellectual Property

Each party retains ownership of pre-existing intellectual property. Ownership, licensing, publication, and access rules for jointly developed results must be defined in a separate project-specific instrument.

Autonomy

This Memorandum does not create a partnership, joint venture, employment relationship, agency, exclusivity arrangement, or authority for one party to bind the other.
