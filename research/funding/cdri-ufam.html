Manaus Urban Infrastructure Resilience

Working concept for joint development
c-ECO Institute

Mission. To advance research, education, scientific cooperation, and decision-support instruments for infrastructure resilience, climate adaptation, disaster risk reduction, and evidence-based public governance.

Vision. Public institutions and infrastructure systems capable of anticipating risk, integrating scientific evidence, and adopting transparent, adaptive, and public-interest decisions.

Executive Summary
The preliminary concept is an urban infrastructure decision-support protocol that integrates existing hydrological, climate, exposure, and service data and converts them into predefined institutional actions for Manaus.

This is not a generic environmental monitoring project. It is an infrastructure-management initiative supported by data. The project would focus on technical assistance, applied research, capacity building, institutional integration, and open project results rather than physical works or a complex new technology platform.

Priority Territory

Manaus is proposed as the initial priority territory because of its population, national economic relevance, river-dependent logistics, exposure to droughts and floods, and current planning processes.

  • Potential institutional entry points: master plan review, climate adaptation, drainage, emergency management, and infrastructure investment planning.
  • Potential additional validation areas: Manacapuru, Tabatinga, or Tefé, subject to partners, logistics, and endorsements.
  • The proposal should remain viable as a single-city project.
Problem Formulation

Weak framing: “Manaus needs a protocol to monitor river levels.”

Stronger framing: “The agencies responsible for urban planning, drainage, mobility, water supply, sanitation, and continuity of essential services in Manaus lack a common institutional process that converts hydrological forecasts and risk data into predefined infrastructure decisions.”

Draft Memorandum of Understanding

UFAM–c-ECO Cooperation Framework

Scientific, technical, educational, and institutional cooperation

Discussion draft only. This text is subject to UFAM’s internal review, legal analysis, competent-authority approval, and adaptation to the University’s official cooperation template.

Parties

Federal University of Amazonas — UFAM, represented by its competent authority.

c-ECO Institute, a nonprofit scientific and educational institution organized in Missouri, United States, represented by its President, Jacqueline Goncalves Alves.

Purpose

The purpose of this Memorandum is to establish a preliminary framework for scientific, technical, educational, and institutional cooperation in areas of mutual interest, including infrastructure resilience, climate adaptation, disaster risk reduction, environmental governance, decision-support systems, artificial intelligence, and public-sector capacity building.

Initial CDRI Cooperation

The parties may jointly assess and develop a proposal under the CDRI Urban Infrastructure Resilience Programme — Brazil 2026, including concept development, methodology, Theory of Change, work planning, stakeholder engagement, budgeting, governance, sustainability, and proposal documentation.

Role of the CCA

Following referral by the UFAM Rector’s Office, the Center for Environmental Sciences may serve as the academic focal point for initial technical discussions, subject to the University’s internal approvals and formal institutional designation.

Financial Understanding

This Memorandum does not provide for the transfer of funds, procurement, employment, or binding financial commitments. Any funded activity must be governed by a separate agreement and approved work plan.

Intellectual Property

Each party retains ownership of pre-existing intellectual property. Ownership, licensing, publication, and access rules for jointly developed results must be defined in a separate project-specific instrument and aligned with funder requirements and UFAM policy.

Autonomy

This Memorandum does not create a partnership, joint venture, employment relationship, agency, exclusivity arrangement, or authority for one party to bind the other.

Term and Termination

The proposed term is 24 months. Either party may terminate the Memorandum by written notice, without affecting separate project agreements already in force.
