// Program Overview

What the Fellowship Is

The c-ECO Fellowship is a formation system built around a live case. Every module produces a real output. Every output feeds into a governance instrument that is tested, reviewed, and — where conditions are met — submitted for institutional use.

It is
  • A live technical formation system operating on real governance cases
  • A structured method for producing legally valid pre-threshold instruments
  • A certification process with 24-month institutional validity
  • A conditioned entry into the c-ECO operational system for applied cases
  • A formation system that generates institutional outputs from day one
It is not
  • A lecture series or theory-only course
  • A certification of knowledge without demonstrated application
  • An entry-level environmental or legal training program
  • A platform for credentials without operative mandate
  • An online course with pre-recorded material and passive consumption

// System Architecture

Four-Layer Formation System

The Fellowship operates through a structured progression. Each layer builds on the previous — access to higher layers is conditional on demonstrated output from the layer below.

Layer 01 · Entry
Foundation Formation
Intensive 8-week formation covering the c-ECO Framework, TDR methodology, TFP instrument logic, and sector-specific governance application. Module sequence is fixed; outputs are cumulative.
All Fellows · Required for certification
Layer 02 · Applied
Case Development
Fellows develop a live governance case — a real system under threshold pressure — applying the full c-ECO methodological sequence. Case is reviewed by the technical team and submitted for institutional assessment.
Conditional on Layer 01 completion and case approval
Layer 03 · Contractual
Instrument License
Fellows whose cases meet the viability threshold may license the resulting governance instrument through the c-ECO system. The license activates the instrument for institutional deployment under defined conditions.
Conditional on case approval · License fee applies
Layer 04 · Advisory
Institutional Advisory
Certified Fellows with active case licenses may be invited into the c-ECO advisory system — providing ongoing institutional input on cases, methodology, and governance design in their sector.
By institutional invitation · Post-certification

// Admission

Admission & Participation

The Fellowship does not admit credentials. It admits cases.

Case Viability Principle. Admission to the c-ECO Fellowship is determined by the viability of the applicant's proposed governance case — not by academic credentials, seniority, or institutional affiliation. The admissions process evaluates whether the proposed case presents: a real system under threshold pressure; a governance gap addressable through the c-ECO methodology; and an applicant with sufficient proximity to the case to produce valid outputs. A strong case with an unconventional applicant is admitted. A weak case with a strong credential is not.

Fellowship Admission USD 1,500 – 2,500

Covers the full 8-week intensive formation, module access, technical review sessions, and issuance of c-ECO Fellowship certification. Fee is fixed per cohort; sliding scale applies for institutional partners.

Case Instrument License USD 5,000 – 50,000+

Applicable only where the developed case meets institutional deployment threshold. License activates the resulting governance instrument for operational use. Fee is determined by case scope, sector, and deployment jurisdiction.
