Research

Active research projects, methodological foundations, and collaboration opportunities across c-ECO's core research domains.

// Active Projects
Threshold Proximity Formalisation

Development of the formal mathematical framework for threshold proximity estimation across domain classes — ecological, financial, and socio-institutional. Producing the algebraic foundations for TDR indicator architecture and TFP activation logic.

Lead: TDR Programme · research@c-eco.io
Active
Amazon Frontier Governance Diagnostics

Systematic legal and institutional mapping of the Amazon frontier governance landscape — authority structures, enforcement gaps, indigenous rights systems, and inter-institutional coordination failures.

Lead: Amazon Living Lab · lab@c-eco.io
Active
TFP Instrument Specification

Drafting and field-testing of the first generation of TFP legal instruments — activation triggers, instrument scope, authority allocation, and deactivation conditions for the tropical biome context.

Lead: TFP Programme · tfp@c-eco.io
Active
Pre-Threshold Legal Architecture

Comparative legal analysis of existing instruments across jurisdictions for evidence of pre-threshold governance logic — identifying precedents, gaps, and design principles applicable to c-ECO Framework institutional architecture.

Lead: c-ECO Framework Programme · research@c-eco.io
Active
Indigenous Governance & Threshold Science Integration

Research programme integrating indigenous ecological knowledge systems with TDR threshold detection methods — developing hybrid monitoring frameworks that combine traditional knowledge with quantitative threshold models.

Lead: Amazon Living Lab · lab@c-eco.io
Active
CDRI Proposal PMO Dashboard

Funding coordination dashboard for CDRI proposal development, consortium workstreams, deliverables, milestones, risks, and decision tracking under the c-ECO Institute model.

Area: Research Funding · Open dashboard

// Research Approach
Method
Mixed-Methods

Quantitative modelling combined with qualitative legal analysis — no single discipline provides sufficient tools for threshold governance research.

Validation
Field-Validated

All models tested against Amazon Living Lab data. Framework designs inform what the Lab measures; Lab data refines what the frameworks specify.

Scope
Interdisciplinary

Ecology, law, mathematics, governance science — synthesis across disciplines is not optional in threshold governance research.

Orientation
Applied

Outputs designed for institutional operationalisation. Research that cannot translate to governance instruments is out of scope.

// Collaborate

c-ECO welcomes research collaboration proposals from institutions and individuals with expertise relevant to threshold governance, ecological threshold science, legal architecture, or related fields.
