// c-ECO Institute

Research infrastructure for anticipatory governance.

The c-ECO Institute advances scientific, educational, and charitable initiatives that strengthen ecological governance, systemic risk analysis, climate adaptation, disaster risk reduction, artificial intelligence, and public policy innovation for the public benefit.

Mission

Public benefit through scientific governance

To advance scientific, educational, and charitable initiatives that strengthen ecological governance, systemic risk analysis, climate adaptation, disaster risk reduction, artificial intelligence, and evidence-based public policy for the public benefit.

Vision

Before irreversible consequences occur

A world where societies recognize critical ecological and institutional thresholds before irreversible consequences occur, enabling governance that is anticipatory, resilient, and scientifically informed.

Core Values

Institutional commitments

  • Scientific Integrity - Independent, evidence-based research and interdisciplinary scholarship.
  • Public Benefit - Knowledge developed to strengthen society, institutions, and communities.
  • Innovation - New methods and governance frameworks for emerging global challenges.
  • Collaboration - International partnerships across academia, government, civil society, and industry.
  • Stewardship - Responsible management of knowledge, institutions, and ecological systems.

Governance

Transparent and accountable functions

The Institute maintains clear institutional governance by separating board oversight, executive leadership, scientific integrity, compliance, and program administration into transparent and accountable functions.

Collaborate with Us

Strategic partnerships and cooperation

The c-ECO Institute welcomes collaboration with universities, research centers, governments, international organizations, philanthropic institutions, nonprofit organizations, and private-sector partners whose missions align with the advancement of scientific knowledge and public benefit.

Organizations interested in developing collaborative projects or strategic partnerships are invited to contact the Institute to explore opportunities for cooperation.

  • Joint research projects
  • Grant-funded research consortia
  • Scientific and technical cooperation
  • Living laboratories and pilot programs
  • Academic exchanges and fellowships
  • Capacity-building and executive education
  • Public policy and governance innovation
  • Artificial intelligence and decision-support research
